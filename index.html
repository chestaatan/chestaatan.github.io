While the wide range in the scope of work that I do keeps me exposed to different management requirements and technical skillsets, I enjoy the unique challenges that each task poses.

This list does not showcase all the projects that I had the privilege to work on but it serves to highlight the breadth of my experience in design and management. I also greatly enjoyed the experience when I was working on them.

Outdoor Surveillance Robot

POSITION:
PROJECT LEAD

The Hecate surveillance robot is an outdoor robot designed to patrol open outdoor areas with low light and footfall. With an innovative threat detection system that combines mechanical and artificial intelligence solutions designed in Solidworks, PyTorch and Scikit, the robot can successfully detect intruders in pitch darkness up to 100m away. An electrically modular design for the robot allows for different mission modules to be switched out easily to accomodate various different use cases.

Pipsqueak

POSITION:
CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER

Pipsqueak is a cross-platform e-commerce site aimed to bring project materials to students at an affordable rate by making bulk purchases directly from manufacturers. With a telegram-integrated chatbot hosted on and Heroku and a website hosted on Alibaba Cloud, customers are able to make purchases hassle-free and can collect their parts on the same day at various secure drop-off points located around the school.

Potato Pirates

POSITION:
GRAPHIC DESIGNER

Potato Pirates is a game series aimed at teaching children of age 6 and up coding and computing concepts with the help of a card game. With a bright and high-contrast style for the unique characters drawn with Clip Studio Paint, compelling storyline told through video on Adobe Premiere and an engaging play-mechanism refined through multiple play-tests, it allowed the game to reach international recognition where it clinched the Singapore Good Design Award, the Japan Good Design Award and the Core77 Design Award.

A second game was also subsequently made and launched in 2020, but with a focus on cybersecurity and networking.
