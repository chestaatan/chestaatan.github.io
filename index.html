My industry experience in design management has been an extremely varied one as I seek out projects that pique my interest to work on.

Music Staircase

POSITION:
PROJECT MANAGER

The Music Staircase is an interactive installation located on the steps of Hard Rock Cafe Singapore at Cuscaden Road. A project done independently, the rugged, waterproof units designed with Fusion 360 for low-volume manufacturing transform the 17 outdoor steps outside the restaurant into an original rhythm-based based game console. C+ and Python scripts are written to give different song game maps, and the setup is electrically wired for Serial control to ensure scaleability.

Outdoor Surveillance Robot

POSITION:
PROJECT LEAD

The Hecate surveillance robot is an outdoor robot designed to patrol open outdoor areas with low light and footfall. With an innovative threat detection system that combines mechanical and artificial intelligence solutions designed in Solidworks, PyTorch and Scikit, the robot can successfully detect intruders in pitch darkness up to 100m away. An electrically modular design for the robot allows for different mission modules to be switched out easily to accomodate various different use cases.

Pipsqueak

POSITION:
CHIEF EXECUTIVE OFFICER

Pipsqueak is a cross-platform e-commerce site aimed to bring project materials to students at an affordable rate by making bulk purchases directly from manufacturers. With a telegram-integrated chatbot hosted on Heroku and a website hosted on Alibaba Cloud, customers are able to make purchases hassle-free and can collect their parts on the same day at various secure drop-off points located around the school.

Potato Pirates

POSITION:
GRAPHIC DESIGNER

Potato Pirates is a game series aimed at teaching children of age 6 and up coding and computing concepts with the help of a card game. With a bright and high-contrast style for the unique characters drawn with Clip Studio Paint, compelling storyline told through video on Adobe Premiere and an engaging play-mechanism refined through multiple play-tests, it allowed the game to reach international recognition where it clinched the Singapore Good Design Award, the Japan Good Design Award and the Core77 Design Award.

A second game was also subsequently made and launched in 2020, but with a focus on cybersecurity and networking.

PERSONAL PROJECTS

Aside from work done with companies, I enjoy picking up projects to work on whenever I have the time to do so. With no time restrictions on the work that I do for a hobby, I am able to pick up many different skillsets on the side that I would otherwise not be able to be exposed to.

Below is just a small list of the projects that I have picked up and am particularly proud of:

Radio-Controlled Speedboat

As an experiment to try out several new features that Solidworks has, a speedboat was designed in order to understand various lesser-used functions such as surface modelling. Extensive research was put into hull design and while no complex speedboat hull plans could be found online for referencing, a combination of real-life photos and theoretical knowledge gleaned from hull design guides online resulted in the final model. The 3D object was then 3D-printed, waterproofed and finished before the electronics, motor and other moving parts were installed.

Direct Modeling & Printing

ZBrush was self-taught in this project in order explore and better understand the principles of non-parametric modelling and printing. Throughout the course of the project, various 3D models were sculpted and then printed with various printers (Fused Deposition Modelling, Stereolithography Apparatus and Selective Laser Sintering printers were utilised) in order to determine the optimal tpe and settings required for printing such models.

Afterwards, various post-processing and finishing methods were explored in order to try and achieve a high-quality finish on the models that has the least amount of artifacts on the surface.
